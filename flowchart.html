About this methodology

A hazard-based risk assessment must always start with the hazard — the stable, ever-present source of potential harm — and trace forward through a realistic causal pathway to identify credible consequences.

Controls (Step 5) are only meaningful once the hazard, pathway, scenario, and consequence are firmly established. Controls that cannot be anchored to a specific step along the causal pathway are invalid.

Starting the assessment at Step 4 (consequence-first) breaks the logical chain, prevents proper SFARP demonstration, and must not proceed.
